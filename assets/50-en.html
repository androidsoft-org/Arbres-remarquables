This oak (Quercus petraea, Liebl.), Planted in 1784, is remarkable access: its age and its port. It belongs to the family Fagaceae, native to Western Europe.

In 2011, this tree was 30 meters high with a trunk circumference of 4.30 meters.

Native to temperate Europe, Caucasus and Asia Minor, rare in the Mediterranean region.

Alternate Name: Sessile / female / black / drill

Petraea means' that grows in rocky places; Quercus sessiliflora also called for the flowers, then fruits are sessile - no peduncle.

Oak, from the Latin "robur," strength, vigor, strength, which designated the oak hardwood.

This is mainly a forest species.
