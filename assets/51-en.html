This Montpellier maple (Acer monspessulanum, L.), planted in 1883, is remarkable for its age. It belongs to the Sapindaceae family, native to southern Europe.

In 2011, this tree was 12 meters high with a trunk circumference of 2.25 meters.

A native of southern Europe and parts of Western Europe to the Caucasus. Abundant in warm, rocky places around Montpellier.

Identified by the Bauhin brothers from the sixteenth century.

Small tree rarely exceeding 12 meters with a rounded crown. It is resistant to cold, hardy in the climate of Paris.
