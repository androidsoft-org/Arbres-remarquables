This chestnut of India (Aesculus hippocastanum L.) is remarkable for its impressive harbor.

Family Sapindaceae, is native to the Balkans.

In 2011, this tree was 30 meters high with a trunk circumference of 5.10 meters.

Hometown: the Balkans and Asia Minor. The first French foot was planted in Paris in 1615 from an original plan of Constantinople.

Etymology: Latin name Aesculus = a holm oak acorns edible dedicated to Jupiter

hippocastanum = from the Latin 'horse chestnut'

In town this tree suffers much from drought and aridity atmospheric causes yellowing of the leaves from July to August.

Since 2001 the chestnut is the victim of an insect called the horse chestnut leaf miner, which burrows in the leaves and weakening the tree. For this reason some chestnut foliage has a red during the summer.
