This common plane (Platanus x acerifolia, Willd.), Planted in 1880, is remarkable for its height and age. Hybrid between the oriental plane tree and the plane of the West, it belongs to the family of Platanaceae.

In 2011, this tree was 20 meters high with a trunk circumference of 3.95 meters.

Platanus x hispanica also called.

Etymology: platanus = from the Greek platys, broad and flat (an allusion to the shape of leaves)

acerifolia = from the Latin 'Maple Leaf'

Unknown in the wild, it is considered to be a hybrid between Platanus occidentalis L. North America and Platanus orientalis L. from the Balkans, an old cultivated form of the latter.

This is one of the trees that best supports the air pollution and severe pruning. Fruit, characteristic of the genus Platanus, hairy achenes are pressed against each other. They form a compact ball that lasts until spring. Fruit (achene) irritant (mechanical): itching powder.

The bark peels off.

Parisian culture of clones is problematic in the medium term (see anthracnose, canker plane) of the health point of view.
