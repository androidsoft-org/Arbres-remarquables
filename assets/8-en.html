The cork oak (Quercus suber L.), planted in 1895, is remarkable for its rarity and its age.

Of the family Fagaceae, is native to Mediterranean Europe.

In 2011, this tree was 10 meters high with a trunk circumference of 1.80 meters.

A native of the Mediterranean.

It produces a thick gray bark and cracked popuvant reach 25cm thick.

Suber means cork in Latin.

Cork is used for making corks but also for thermal insulation.

This species is rare in Paris.
