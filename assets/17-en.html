This alder (Alnus glutinosa, Gaertn.), Planted in 1933, is remarkable for its height and its port. It belongs to the family Betulaceae, native to Europe.

In 2011, this tree was 16 meters high with a trunk circumference of 2.20 meters.

Originally from Europe to the Caucasus to Asia Minor and Siberia.

"Glutinous" by referring to the viscosity of the buds and young leaves in spring.

It is called "verne" south of the Loire and sometimes red alder because of the color of its wood.

This species likes mainly in swampy areas.
