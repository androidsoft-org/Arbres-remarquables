The giant sequoia (Sequoiadendron giganteum Buchholz) is remarkable for its age. Of the family Taxodiaceae, a native of the West Coast of the United States.

In 2011, this tree was 35 meters high with a trunk circumference of 4.70 meters.

Native to North America (Nevada and California).

Pyrophile plant (plants whose germination is favored by fire)

Redwoods are also the giants of the vegetable kingdom.

Sequoia comes from the Cherokee "Sequoyah," a term meaning 'possum', nickname of a Metis Indian chief who became famous among his people by inventing the Cherokee alphabet.
