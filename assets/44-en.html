The gutta-percha tree (Eucomimia ulmoides, Oliver) is remarkable for its rarity.

It belongs to the family of Eucomiaceae, native to North America.

In 2011, this tree was 12 meters tall with trunks of 1.90 meters in circumference.

Native to China. Introduced in France in 1896.

Curious in that it contains, in its bark and the ribs of its leaves, a rubber-like gum. This gum is not abundant enough to be exploited.

In China, its bark has medicinal applications.
