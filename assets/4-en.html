The bald cypress (Taxodium distichum, Rich.), Planted in 1862, is remarkable for its age and its port. Of the family Taxodiaceae, he comes from the Gulf of Mexico.

In 2011, this tree was 35 meters high with a trunk circumference of 3.50 meters.

A native of some southern marsh areas of the United States, producing a very special wood, with characteristic pungent odor. Grown in France from the late seventeenth century as an ornamental because of its port and tapered bronze foliage turning red in autumn.

As he grows in swampy or flooded often it has the property of emitting around age 25 to 30 years of pneumatophores (vertical protuberance shaped knees that grow on the roots and out of the ground around the tree) , true respiratory system root.

Its very hard wood, rot, insect resistant is prized for construction.
