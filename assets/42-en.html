This blue Atlas cedar (Cedrus atlantica 'Glauca', Quarry), planted in 1939, is remarkable for its planting to mark the sesquicentennial of the French Revolution. Of the Pinaceae, a native of the Atlas.

In 2011, this tree was 25 meters high with a trunk circumference of 3.50 meters.

Originally from North Africa and especially the Middle Atlas of Morocco. Its erect branches and short needles can be distinguished from its congeners. It was increased from 1839 in France. Glauca is a cultivar with bluish foliage
