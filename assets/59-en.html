This incense cedar (Calocedrus decurrens Florin), planted in 1854, is remarkable for its size and age.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae, native to western United States.

In 2011, this tree was 20 meters high with a trunk circumference of 1.95 meters.

Originally from the West Coast Of U.S..

Introduced in England in 1853.

Other names: Calocèdre / Libocèdre.

Calocedrus means "beautiful cedar", its wood is fragrant like cedar.

The wood used to make pencils in America.
