This Pterocarya of China (Pterocarya stenopptera, DC.), Planted in 1905, is remarkable for its age and size. Of the family Juglandaceae, a native of China.

In 2011, this tree was 30 meters high with a trunk circumference of 5.30 meters.

Chinese native rainforest. Introduced in the West since 1860.

This tree is very impressive family of drowning. Its bark is divided into dark crevices and thick. Can be seen on the trunk of this tree of large bumps called burls. These growths have woody fibers arranged haphazardly.

Better known in cabinet as the "magnifying glass", wood burls is prized for its hardness, its appearance very veined and beautiful colors.
