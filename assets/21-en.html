The cedar of Lebanon (Cedrus Libanii, Rich.), Planted in 1862, is remarkable for its height and age. Of the Pinaceae, he is originally from Lebanon.

In 2011, this tree was 30 meters high with a trunk circumference of 4.60 meters.

Originally from Lebanon, introduced in France in 1734 by Bernard de Jussieu.

It is recognizable by its port tabular becoming older.

Emblem of Lebanon, it is found on their flag.

Tree famous since antiquity where biblical noted that it would have served in the temple of Solomon.
