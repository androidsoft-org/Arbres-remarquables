This elm (Ulmus carpinifolia, Mill.), Planted in 1935, revives the memory of elm around which the Middle Ages, were paying claims. It belongs to the family Ulmaceae native to Europe.

In 2011, this tree was 15 meters high with a trunk circumference of 1.80 meters.

Native to Europe, North Africa and Western Asia. Also known elm leaf charm. This genus has been largely decimated in Paris by the outbreak of elm disease in the 1970s. The large wound extending longitudinally of the foot into the crown was caused by lightning. Planted in memory of the famous elm around which the local people, the Middle Ages, gathered to pay their debt.
