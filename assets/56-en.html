This locust false acacia (Robinia pseudoacacia L.) was planted in 1601 by Henri IV's gardener, Jean Robin, who introduced it in France and gave it its name. It is the oldest tree in Paris. It belongs to the family Fabaceae, native to southeastern United States. In 2011, this tree was 11 meters high with a trunk circumference of 3.85 meters.

Native to eastern United States. Dedicated to Jean Robin, gardener to Henry IV, who introduced the plant in France (Jardin des Plantes, Square Viviani) = pseudoacacia locust because this tree has thorns, fruit pods and leaves imparipinnately compound, like other species of the genus Acacia. This tree is suitable for poor soils. It is honey, its wood is used for grape stakes as very hard and resistant to decay, allows the determination of slope as it suckers a lot, it has become naturalized and became invasive.

Made into fritters (shake well before the cluster of the dip in the batter to knock insects).

The flower essence is used in perfumery.
