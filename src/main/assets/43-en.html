This false Verzy (Fagus sylvatica 'Tortuosa', L.), planted in 1905, is remarkable for its age and its port tortuous. Belonging to the family Fagaceae, is native to the region of Reims. In 2011, this tree was 10 meters high with a trunk circumference of 2.50 meters.

Originally from Verzy south of Reims in the Marne where one finds the largest number of beech trees. It rarely exceeds four to five meters high. The Faux are characterized by slow growth, tormented and durability. The word "fau" meant in Old French beech and Sylvatica for forestry. It is assumed that the fall of a radioactive meteor would have caused this change centuries ago.
