This tree crowns to forty (Ginkgo Boloba, L.), planted in 1879, is remarkable for its age and its projections that descend toward the ground.

It belongs to the family of Ginkgoaceae, native to China.

In 2011, this tree was 22 meters high with a trunk circumference of 2.85 meters.

Native to China.

Etymology: name = Chinese ginkgo tree meaning 'silver apricot'.

biloba from Latin = 'two-lobe' (referring to the shape of the leaves).

Introduced in Europe in 1727, the Ginkgo biloba are the last representatives of a family appeared in the Mesozoic era. Unique, this species, called dioecious, has feet of males and females separated feet, which bloom only after 45 to 50 years.

On the male tree, the flowers appear in spring, met in long drooping catkins. In autumn, leaves fall from the male tree often precedes that of the female tree.

This particular topic has what are called "chi-chis," that is to say protrusions that descend toward the ground (reminiscent of aerial roots).
