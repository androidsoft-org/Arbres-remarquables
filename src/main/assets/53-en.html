The yew tree (Taxus baccata, L.), planted in 1772, is remarkable for its age and its port.

Of the family Taxaceae, it is native to Europe.

In 2011, this tree was 13 meters high with a trunk circumference of 2.35 meters.

From Northern Africa; Southwest Asia, Europe.

The fruit of the yew is a red berry called "arils". All plant parts contain a highly toxic alkaloid taxine except fruit pulp.

This is one of the trees most resistant to urban pollution.

It is often used in topiary in French gardens and cemeteries.

The yew wood was used for making bows and crossbows.

The yew is a symbol of life and death. Because of its longevity (it can exceed more than 2000 years) it is linked to immortality.
