This silver linden (Tilia tomentosa Moench), planted in 1945, is remarkable for its planting to mark the liberation of Paris. Of the family Malvaceae, is native to the eastern Mediterranean.

In 2011, this tree was 20 meters high with a trunk circumference of 2.10 meters.

Originally from South-East Europe, Asia Minor, introduced in France in 1794 at the Jardin des Plantes in Paris.

Another name Tilia Argentea, silver in Latin because the underside of leaves has a whitish pubescence, where a tomentum "tomentosa" which also covers the buds and twigs.

Beautiful tree alignment along an ornamental for its leaves to the changing aspect.
