This evergreen redwood (Sequoia sempervirens, Endl.), Planted in 1935, is remarkable for its port and its height. It belongs to the family Taxodiaceae.

In 2011, this tree was 30 meters high with a trunk circumference of 3.35 meters.

Originally from the chain of coastal southern Oregon to California.

Also called redwood leaf Yew or redwood.

Sequoia comes from the Cherokee "Sequoyah," a term meaning 'possum', nickname of a Metis Indian chief who became famous among his people by inventing the Cherokee alphabet.

Its thick bark reddish orange allows him to withstand fire.

This is one of the highest trees in the world.
