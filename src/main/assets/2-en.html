The copper beech (Fagus sylvatica 'Purpurea', L.) is remarkable for its port and its circumference.

It belongs to the family Fagaceae, native to Europe.

In 2011, this tree was 23 meters high with a trunk circumference of 3.70 meters.

The copper beech is known since the early seventeenth century. That of the forest hanleiter in Thuringia (Germany) discovered in 1680 is the source of all ornamental trees of this variety grown since then.
