This ailanthus (Ailanthus giraldii, Dode) is remarkable for its impressive shape.

Of the family Simaroubaceae, it is native to China.

In 2011, this tree was 35 meters high with a trunk circumference of 4.60 meters.

A native of western China. Discovered in 1895 by Father G. Giraldi Italian missionary who collected many new plants in Shensi.

The family name (Simaroubaceae) comes from Simaruba amara, the Caribbean white wood, with a bitter bark, tonic and febrifuge.
