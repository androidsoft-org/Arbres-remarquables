This Love of Phellodendron (Phellodendron amurense Rupr.), Planted in 1925, is remarkable for its circumference. It belongs to the Rutaceae family native to the shores of the Amur River in the north-east Asia.

In 2011, this tree was 14 meters high with a trunk circumference of 1.80 meters.

Originally from Siberia, China, Manchuria. The genus is represented by nine species. Introduced in Western Europe in 1856.

Tree of Love in Cork (the big river in Asia).

Flower honey. In autumn the foliage turns a beautiful yellow color. The leaves and fruits are fragrant, fruit inedible.

Its large longitudinal wound on the trunk is the result of a lightning strike.
