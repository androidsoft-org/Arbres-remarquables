This hazel (Corylus colurna, L.), planted in 1882, is remarkable for its age.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is native to southeastern Europe.

In 2011, this tree was 20 meters high with a trunk circumference of 2.45 meters.

From the Balkans, Asia Minor and the Caucasus. Introduced in Europe in 1852.

This is a close relative of the common hazel (Corylus avellana). Nuts edible bouquets to appear in the Fall.

Colurna designated the hazel in Virgil.
