This Pterocarya Caucasus (Pterocarya fraxinifolia, Spach), planted in 1894, is remarkable for its age and its circumference. It belongs to the family Juglandaceae, from the Caucasus.

In 2011, this tree was 30 meters high with a trunk circumference of 4.45 meters.

From the Caucasus, Armenia and northern Iran.

Reduced by the botanist Andre Michaux in his travels in Persia in 1782. The first specimens were planted in Versailles

This tree is very impressive family of drowning. Its bark is divided into dark crevices and thick. Can be seen on the trunk of large bumps called burls. These growths have woody fibers arranged haphazardly.

Better known in cabinet as the "magnifying glass", wood burls is prized for its hardness, its appearance very veined and beautiful colors.
