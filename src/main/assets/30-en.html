The Japanese pagoda tree (Styphnolobium japonicum, Schott), planted in 1873, is remarkable for its age and its pendulous habit, especially for the species. It belongs to the family Fabaceae, native to northern China.

In 2011, this tree was 10 meters high with a trunk circumference of 3.35 meters.

Originally from China, Korea and cultivated in Japan hence its name. Introduced in France in 1747 at King's garden. Since the tree is popular because of its light and elegant appearance as an ornamental and avenues (among others in Paris).

The young branches are of a beautiful green jade. The leaves of Sophora are the latest to fall in autumn.
