This hazel (Corylus colurna, L.), planted in 1882, is remarkable for its impressive size for a hazelnut. Belonging to the family Betulaceae, it comes from the south-east Europe.

In 2011, this tree was 20 meters high with a trunk circumference of 2.10 meters.

From the Balkans, Asia Minor and the Caucasus. Introduced in Europe in 1852.

This is a close relative of the common hazel (Corylus avellana). Hazelnuts edible bouquets to appear in the Fall.

Colurna designated the hazel in Virgil.
