This paper mulberry (Broussonetia papyrifera, Vent.) Is remarkable for its rarity and its size.

Of the family Moraceae, it is native to Southeast Asia.

In 2011, this tree was 12 meters high with a trunk circumference of 1.90 meters.

Originally from Southeast asaitique. Cultivated in Europe since 1750 for the ornament. The first females were introduced in France in 1786 at the king's garden.

The bark and young twigs are used to make paper hence the name "papyrifera" meaning: that produces paper.

This tree has a "magnifying glass", it is a malformation of the trunk due to reaction of the tree following an assault (injury, insect bite)

This protrusion, sought in cabinetmaking, can make fine furniture (marquetry).
