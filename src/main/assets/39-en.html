This common plane (Platanus x acerifolia botanist, Willd.), Planted in 1840, is remarkable for its height and age. Hybrid between the Oriental plane tree and the plane of the West, it belongs to the family of Platanaceae.

In 2011, this tree was 40 meters high with a trunk circumference of 5.80 meters.

Platanus x hispanica also called.

Etymology: platanus = from the Greek platys, broad and flat (an allusion to the shape of the leaves) from the Latin acerifolia = 'Maple Leaf'

Unknown in the wild, it is considered to be a hybrid between Platanus occidentalis L. North America and Platanus orientalis L. from the Balkans, an old cultivated form of the latter. This is one of the trees that best supports the air pollution and severe pruning. Fruit, characteristic of the genus Platanus, hairy achenes are pressed against each other. They form a compact ball that lasts until spring.

The fruits are irritating.

The bark peels off.
