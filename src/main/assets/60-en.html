The common catalpa (Catalpa bignonioides, Walter) is remarkable for its circumference and its natural shape.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae, native to southwestern United States.

In 2011, this tree was 15 meters high with a trunk circumference of 2.60 meters.

Origin South of the United States, specifically coastal plain of the Gulf of Mexico (Caroline), introduced in France in 1754.

Catalpa "means" bean "in Cherokee language because the fruit is a pod that looks like a long bean.
