This persimmon (Diospyros kaki, Thunb.) Is remarkable for its age and, in winter, for its decorative fruit. It belongs to the family of Ebenaceae, native to China.

In 2011, this tree was 12 meters high with a trunk circumference of 1.60 meters.

Native to China and Japan. Khaki is the Japanese abbreviation of the name of the fruit 'kakino'. It was introduced in Europe in 1796. A large yellow fruit persistent into the winter, edible when ripe. Its fruits are attacked by blackbirds and starlings after the first frosts.
